Desulfurization of Methanol Through Sonolysis

This past year, as my senior research project, I focused on development of a patent system for removing sulphur impurities from a hydrocarbon stream, which we are now applying to desulfurating waste methanol. You can find the original 2004 patent from Dr. Mahajan here. And you can find my working thesis document here.

The project is centered around sultrasonic sonication: a process that produces a phenonemon known as "acoustic cavitation." Effective cavtiation produces vapor filled vacuoles that rapidy form, implode, and reform, generating microscopic hot-spots of intense pressure and temperature, sufficient to strip Fe(CO)5 to iron-0, which will then react with sulphur complexes in solution. I also presented this work to the Chemistry senior research committee; you can find those slides and original artwork here.
